Here

My first ever project, created as a way to reinforce all of the knowledge I had acquired up to that point. As such it does not make use of certain properties I would now consider to be more appropriate. For example, display:grid together with grid-template-areas: would have made placing certain elements on the board more intuitive.

In order to work effectively and maximise my learnings I had limited myself to 2 weeks of working time. I believe this constrain mirrored a real project and allowed me to hone my time management and prioritisation skills.

I have to say that working with JavaScript was an absolute blast! One day I was struggling with re-rendering the board after combat, after 3 hours of checking everything and slowly eliminating potential causes I had finally managed to crack it. I have screamed triumphantly like a child, honestly I had not felt this level of an acomplishment before in my adult life!

This "game" demo was designed with scalability in mind so any feature already implemented can be easily expanded on (such as more heroes, more maps and more varied monsters). I had also paid attention to the responsiveness of the game window for different sized screens, and whilst I may not happy with the final result I have learned a great deal from simply attempting it.
